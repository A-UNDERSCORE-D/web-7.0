Help protect net neutrality in the EU

 edk and Swant on 2016-07-04

Freen­ode users, in a few days the EU will de­cide on net neu­tral­i­ty, the out­come will af­fect all of us, not only Eu­ro­peans, and it will def­i­nite­ly harm FOSS.

As many of you no doubt know, the Eu­ro­pean Union passed leg­is­la­tion last year in­tend­ed to pro­tect net neu­tral­i­ty. How­ev­er, it con­tained sig­nif­i­cant loop­holes which many peo­ple be­lieve will al­low Eu­ro­pean ISPs to pri­ori­tise pay­ing part­ner­s' traf­fic.

This will ben­e­fit those will­ing, and able, to pay. Many FOSS projects does­n't have an in­come, and those who does have of­ten gains just enough to cov­er host­ing costs, in com­par­i­son to mul­ti bil­lion­aire for-prof­it cor­po­rates that have hun­dreds, if not thou­sands of dol­lars to pay your IS­P's to pri­or­i­tize their con­tent, FOSS will soon be mar­gin­alised, be­cause in the end, peo­ple will choose a fast ser­vice over a slow one.

If you live out­side EU this can af­fect you as well, if FOSS ser­vices get mar­gin­alised in the EU, it won't be long un­til the rest of the world, for com­pat­i­bil­i­ty choose the same ser­vice.

If this is some­thing that con­cerns you, cam­paigns aimed at con­vinc­ing BEREC to close the loop­holes are run­ning at https://www.savenet­neu­tral­i­ty.eu/ and https://savethein­ter­net.eu/en/.
